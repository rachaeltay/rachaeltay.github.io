Despite the fatigue from finishing my examinations, I felt energized as I was excited to be an ambassador at Innovfest Unbound. In order to to be a student ambassador, I had to write in my reasons as to why I wanted to join, how I wanted to contribute and how I'm going to achieve that. After being shortlisted and interviewed, I was part of the team. My role was to guide a guest speaker assigned to us throughout the event to ensure they get to their speaking location on time. On top of that, I would be coordinating and facilitating a sharing session called mini Kopi Chat for other attendees of the event to interact with our speaker. This is to provide an opportunity for the speaker to share his/her vast experience and insight on industry trends.

I learnt so much from the speaker assigned to me, Stuart Thornton. He is the Vice President for Business Development in WorldPay and an advisor for multiple startups. I was amazed from the way he spoke to the way he acted. In hindsight, maybe it was due to my lack of experience in interacting with high profile business people. Nevertheless, he gave me a glimpse into how a genuine leader would act in favor of choosing to serve and share rather than just lead. I look forward to attending more of such events to learn more from leaders in their various fields and exploring where my own interests may lie in.
